Basics

Understand the filing label before you open a state page

States use different names for recurring compliance tasks. Match the label first so you open the right deadline, fee, and official filing page.

Quick label guide

Label What it usually means Example on this site Main risk if you mix it up
Annual report A yearly filing that confirms entity information or continued existence with the state. North Carolina and Pennsylvania You may miss a filing window or rely on the wrong fee schedule.
Annual registration A yearly entity registration update that serves a similar recurring-compliance role but uses a different state label. Georgia You may search for "annual report" and never reach the right Georgia portal.
Annual list A yearly list filing that may be paired with another renewal fee instead of standing alone. Nevada You may overlook the separate state business license renewal fee.
Annual tax A recurring state charge that may apply even when there is no annual report for that entity type. Delaware LLC annual tax You may think no action is due because you searched only for "annual report."
Franchise tax report A yearly tax filing that can come with separate information-report requirements and tax thresholds. Texas You may miss the information report or misunderstand no-tax-due filing rules.

Before you use a guide

  • Match the state and entity type first.
  • Use the label the state publishes, not the label a third-party article uses.
  • Check whether the obligation is a filing, a tax, a registration, or a combination.
  • Finish on the official state page before you file or pay.

Quick questions

Question What is an annual report?

An annual report is usually a recurring state filing that confirms entity details or continued authority to do business.

Question Is annual registration the same thing?

Not always. Some states use a different filing label even when the task looks similar on the surface.

Question What should I do next?

Use State compare or open the matching state guide once you know which filing label applies.
