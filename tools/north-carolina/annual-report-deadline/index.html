North Carolina annual report deadline for LLCs and corporations

The North Carolina Secretary of State publishes different annual report timing rules by entity type. LLCs and L3Cs use a fixed April 15 deadline, while business corporations follow the 15th day of the fourth month after the fiscal year ends.

At a glance

LLC or L3C deadline April 15 each year after the year of creation.
LLC fee $203 online or $200 on paper.
Corporation timing 15th day of the fourth month following fiscal year end.

Fee and deadline table

What the North Carolina chart says

Entity type Published due date Online fee Paper fee
Business corporation 15th day of the fourth month following fiscal year end $25 $25
Limited Liability Company (LLC or L3C) April 15 each year after the year of creation $203 $200
Partnerships (LLP and LLLP) 15th day of the fourth month after fiscal year end $203 $200

How to read the deadline correctly

LLCs in North Carolina are simpler than corporations: the chart uses a fixed April 15 due date. Business corporations do not use that fixed date because the filing deadline tracks the entity's fiscal year end instead.

What happens if you are late

The annual report due-date chart confirms when the filing is due and what the filing fee is, but it does not publish a flat late fee on the same page. If you are already overdue, check the Secretary of State filing instructions before relying on any third-party summary.
